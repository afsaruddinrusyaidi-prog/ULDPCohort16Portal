FAQ

The answers we can give you — and a few honest "you'll see"s.

The programme

What exactly is ULDP?
The University Leadership Development Programme is Axiata Foundation's flagship leadership experience for Malaysian undergraduates, under the Axiata Young Talent Programme (AYTP). Cohort 16 brings together ~80 selected students for a virtual bootcamp (18–21 July), a residential bootcamp at Espira Kinrara (23–31 July), and a pre-programme experience that starts well before.
Does it cost anything?
No — the programme, accommodation and meals during the residential bootcamp are fully sponsored by Axiata Foundation. You handle your own travel to and from the venue on travel day.
Who runs the sessions?
ThinkCodex, our learning partner, designs and delivers the academic programme — with Axiata leaders joining throughout, and the Axiata Foundation team running everything around it.

The houses

How am I sorted into a House?
The programme team curates every House to be balanced and diverse — a deliberate mix of strengths, campuses and characters. The exact method is a programme secret. All four Houses are announced together on House Reveal Day, and you'll land where you fit.
Can I switch Houses to be with my friends?
No — and honestly, that's the point. Your House is your chance to build twenty new corners of your network. By graduation, you won't want to switch.
What does the House Captain do?
Each House elects a Captain a few days after the reveal. Captains coordinate official House submissions, run polls and discussions, and represent the House at graduation. Any House member can nominate themselves.

Logistics

Where do I stay during the physical bootcamp?
At Espira Kinrara, Puchong — accommodation is provided for all seven nights (23–30 July), with graduation on the evening of 31 July. Rooming details are shared at check-in.
What should I bring?
Smart-casual wear, one formal outfit for graduation, comfortable shoes, your laptop and chargers, a water bottle, and personal medication. A complete packing list goes to all confirmed participants before travel day.
What do I need for the virtual days?
A stable internet connection, a quiet space, and your camera on. Zoom links and the daily timetable arrive by email and the cohort WhatsApp group before Day 1.

The standings

How do Houses earn points?
Across three fronts — Community, Challenges and Spirit. The specific activities are revealed as the programme unfolds. What we can tell you: showing up, getting involved and backing your House always pays.
What is the House MVP?
Alongside the House race, each member quietly builds an individual contribution tally. The top contributor in each House is crowned House MVP at graduation. Every contribution counts — even when you don't know it's being counted. 👀
Who verifies the points?
Every point on the leaderboard is reviewed and approved by the programme team before it appears, with evidence behind each entry. The board updates throughout the programme — if it just changed, someone earned it.
